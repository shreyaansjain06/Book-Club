Objective

Bring your thoughts and perspectives to a book club or start one of your own.

How

Introduce titles in an existing club or start a new club.

Bottom line

You can introduce “persuadables,” or “switchables,” to conservative ideas

Caveat

Politics can destroy a book club. Be forthright but wise.

Instructions

  1. Learn about book clubs
  2. Join an existing club
  3. Create a conservative club
  4. Hints on interacting

2. Join an existing clubs

If you join an existing club, it may be wise to not suggest books, but rather simply defend conservative thinking. You can also consider using the book The 5000 Year Leap by W. Cleon Skousen, as a way to discuss civics and good governance in a way that is politically neutral.

If you do give input, you may want to suggest scholarly minority authors from the right, such as Thomas Sowell, Shelby Steele and Walter Williams.

4. Some hints on interacting

Watch this video on how to best lead book discussions




You can organize the club and create a forum for it on Facebook or another social media platform. Click here to learn how.

Consider books with a conservative message that are not necessarily partisan or policy oriented.

Consider also discussing some books from the left, such as Saul Alinsky's "Rules for Radicals."


Here are some tips on leading discussions:
  • Take notes as you read and bring them to the meeting
  • Pick out a specific passage from the book and ask members to comment.
  • Support your views, using specific passages from the book.
  • Choose one question at a time to pose to the group.

5. Some possible questions

For fiction books:
  1. Which characters do you admire or dislike?
  2. What motivates the character's actions?
  3. Do any characters grow or change during the course of the novel?
For non-fiction books:
  1. What does the author celebrate or criticize in the culture?
  2. What are the issues raised?
  3. Do the issues affect your life?
  4. What evidence does the author give to support the book's ideas?
  5. Do the issues the book raises have implications for the future?
  6. Does the author make a call to action to readers?
  7. Did you learn something new?
